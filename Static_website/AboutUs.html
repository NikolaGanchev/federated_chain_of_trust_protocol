About Us

This project was created for the FMI Codes 2026 hackathon by The Commiters

The Federated Chain Of Trust Protocol (FCTP) is a proposed framework for verifying identity-related information online in a way that is both privacy-preserving and widely interoperable. Its main goal is to let websites accept verified claims — such as whether a person is over a certain age, or whether they hold a particular nationality — without having to build separate technical integrations with government databases, national identity systems, or commercial identity providers.

At the core of FCTP is the idea of an open trust network. Instead of depending on one central authority, the protocol allows many different kinds of organizations to issue trusted credentials. These issuers can range from large official institutions, like government ministries, to smaller groups, such as local clubs or associations. Trust is organized as a directed graph, meaning that each website can decide which issuers it accepts directly or indirectly through chains of trust.

For users, this design simplifies how credentials are presented. A person does not need to reveal the entire path of their original credential or interact separately with every website. Instead, they can present a single token, obtained through exchange at the highest issuer that the website already trusts, even if the original credential came from deeper within the trust network. This makes verification smoother for websites while also reducing unnecessary data exposure for users.

Importantly, FCTP is not meant to replace existing digital identity systems. It is designed to build on top of current infrastructure, including frameworks such as eIDAS 2.0, so that existing credentials and trust relationships can still be used. The protocol is also token-format agnostic, meaning it can work with multiple credential and token technologies, including Privacy Pass blinded tokens, SD-JWT, mdoc, and others. This flexibility allows it to adapt to different technical environments while keeping the same overall trust model. In short, FCTP provides a way for the internet to support trusted, reusable, and privacy-conscious verification of identity attributes, without forcing every website to reinvent the same identity-checking systems.
